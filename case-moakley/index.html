Case study / 03

Judges chamber renovation, Moakley Courthouse.

Alares provided comprehensive design services for the renovation of a federal judge's chamber at the iconic Moakley Courthouse in Boston. The project aimed to modernize the space while preserving its distinguished aesthetic.

← All case studies
Client

General Services Administration

Location

Moakley Courthouse, Boston, MA

Disciplines

Architecture · MEP · Fire Protection

Scope

Renovation

Detail

Scope of work

Services provided

  • Architectural design. Renovation of existing spaces, including layout updates and aesthetic enhancements
  • Mechanical and structural engineering. HVAC upgrades and structural reinforcement where needed
  • Electrical and fire protection. Design and upgrades in collaboration with New England Engineering
  • Cost estimating. Comprehensive cost analysis and budgeting for all design phases
  • Documentation. Concept designs, detailed designs and bid-ready documents

Design highlights

Renovated judges chamber

  • Private bathroom addition integrated into the judge's chamber for improved functionality
  • Architectural upgrades. New millwork trim, wainscoting panels and cased openings
  • Replacement of doors, carpet, paint and ceilings

Selective demolition

Removal of existing ceilings, lighting, walls and flooring to accommodate new design elements.

Systems upgrades

Comprehensive updates to mechanical, electrical and fire protection systems to meet modern standards.

Key outcomes

  • Modernized space. A balance of modern functionality and classic aesthetics, respecting the building's legacy.
  • Improved building systems. Mechanical, electrical and fire protection upgrades enhanced safety, comfort and efficiency.
  • Comprehensive documentation. Detailed bid documents and cost estimates supported a smooth construction process.
  • Client satisfaction. Thoughtful design and attention to detail earned positive feedback from stakeholders.
