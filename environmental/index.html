Environmental services.

Capabilities / 01 / 05

Five disciplines, one accountable team.

01
Site investigation

Phase I and Phase II Environmental Site Assessments to assess environmental conditions and identify potential contaminants, crucial for informed decisions in property transactions and development projects.

  • Historical research and records review
  • Soil, groundwater and surface water sampling
  • Risk assessment and reporting in formats accepted by regulators and lenders
02
Remediation

Tailored strategies to manage and mitigate environmental hazards. We aim to restore sites to safe and usable conditions, ensuring compliance with environmental regulations.

  • Design and implementation of soil and groundwater remediation systems
  • In-situ and ex-situ treatment technologies matched to site conditions
  • Long-term monitoring and maintenance to verify ongoing compliance
03
Demolition design & hazmat

Planning and executing demolition projects with safety and environmental responsibility at the center.

  • Asbestos and lead-based paint surveys
  • Mold and radon assessments
  • Safe removal and disposal of hazardous substances
  • Structural assessments and demolition plans
  • Oversight and management of demolition activities
04
NEPA services

Compliance with the National Environmental Policy Act is essential for federal projects. We guide clients through the process to ensure informed decision-making and regulatory compliance.

  • Environmental Assessments (EAs)
  • Environmental Impact Statements (EISs)
  • Public involvement and scoping
  • Mitigation planning
05
Section 106 compliance

Section 106 of the National Historic Preservation Act mandates consideration of historic properties in federal project planning. We help clients meet that requirement while keeping the project moving.

  • Identification and evaluation of historic properties
  • Assessment of project effects on cultural resources
  • Coordination with State Historic Preservation Offices and other stakeholders
  • Drafting Memoranda of Agreement (MOAs)
